Brian Joseph / Community Seat. Brian started in Campus radio when he was 17 on John Abbott’s CSKY Radio. After graduating CEGEP, he went into Communications Studies at Concordia University, where in 2004, he started hosting a weekly Hip Hop show on CJLO titled Phantastiq Cypha. Brian would go on to become the Hip Hip Music Director for CJLO and win Special Music Director of the year in 2009. He has represented CJLO at festivals in New York, Toronto, Australia and Zurich. Currently he runs the site FranklinArmstrong.com, which is a hub for geek & pop culture podcasts. Term ending: 2022

Me. Patrice Blais / Faculty Seat. Patrice has been involved with CJLO since his student years in 2001. He has been serving on the Board in various roles initially as a student representative and now as a faculty representative. He is a practicing lawyer since 2006 and a faculty member at Concordia since 2009. He is teaching business law in JMSB and not for profit organizations law in the Political Science department. His priorities are establishing good governance practices and the long term financial planning of CJLO. Term ending: 2021

Alex Viger-Collins / CJLO Seat. Alex hosts the modern pop-oriented show Ashes to Ashes on CJLO 1690AM. A mainstay on the station's schedule since 2015, the show was twice voted as one of Montreal’s best radio shows in Cult MTL's annual Readers Poll. Alex has done over 100 interviews with local and international artists alike, and shared his skills in an interviewing workshop for the station. He contributes to CJLO in numerous ways, including DJing at events, writing pieces for the station's online magazine, and training the next wave of aspiring radio hosts. Term ending: 2021

Angelica Calcagnile / CJLO Student Seat. Angelica has been a member of CJLO 1690AM since 2001, and has contributed to the station in many capacities. Her background is in design and marketing, and her focus on the board is accountability to students, fiscal responsibility, and sustainability with regards to CJLO's future growth in the local community and beyond. Term ending: 2022

Stuart Thiel / Student Seat Stuart Thiel brings a consultative, problem-solving perspective to the board. A Professional Engineer, he values ordered balanced and transparent solutions. He has a history of working with community organizations across Quebec and has developed custom web-radio software and hardware solutions for small community broadcasters. Term ending: 2021

Omar Husain / CJLO Seat. Omar has been a long-time active member at CJLO, hosting "Hooked on Sonics" and formerly sat on the executive body as the Head Music Director up until early 2018. He has extensive management and financial experience and has been in private practice as an accountant and management consultant helping local small businesses, not-for-profits, and self employed individuals & artists sustain and grow their businesses.Term ending: 2022
